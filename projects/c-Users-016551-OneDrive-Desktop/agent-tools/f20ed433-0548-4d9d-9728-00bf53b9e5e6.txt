When I am done walking and getting ready for my swim I put them into MP3 mode. All you have to do to move from bluetooth to MP3 is press the same middle button that you used to turn them on three times very quickly. Once again it will vibrate and you will hear TF mode. That is MP3 mode. (Bluetooth doesn't work in the water if you headphones are submerged for any length of time..) With the MP3 mode on I enjoy listening to music while I am doing my swim. In fact in the water the sound comes through louder and I sometimes have to tun the volume down a little. The unit comes with ear covers that fit inside of your ears to cut down on water noise, but I don't use them and still don't have any trouble hearing my music. If you are just outdoors and fishing or playing in the water you can probably just leave the bluetooth on. But I swim to work out in a pool and if you are doing that you are going to need to go into MP3 mode. You do have to download music to the memory of the MP3 player on your own. If you can't figure out how to do this please just send me a note and I'll be glad to help you out if I can.
