Frequently Asked Questions
Can bone conduction headphones work underwater?
Yes, bone conduction headphones can work underwater. The sound vibrations transmit through the cheek and jaw bones instead of the ear canal. The Mojawa Run Plus can be submerged up to 2 meters underwater for up to 2 hours.
What should I do before swimming with my headphones?
For a better swimming experience, please follow these steps:
1. Disconnect Bluetooth connection on your phone and switch to MP3 mode on your headphones.
2. Disable the touchpad (press 4 times while in "power on" mode).
Can you still hear sound with bone conduction headphones?
Yes, you can still hear sound with bone conduction headphones. The sound vibrations are transmitted through the bones of your skull directly to your inner ear, bypassing the eardrum.
Can deaf people hear sound with bone conduction headphones?
Yes, deaf people can perceive sound through bone conduction headphones because they do not rely on the eardrum to transmit sound. The vibrations directly stimulate the cochlea. However, individuals with certain neurological impairments or disabilities may have different responses, depending on their specific condition and medical advice.
Can other people hear bone-conducted music?
Other people in close proximity may faintly hear the sound coming from bone conduction headphones. However, the sound leakage is much less compared to traditional headphones.
Do bone conduction headphones leak sound?
Bone conduction headphones have minimal sound leakage compared to traditional headphones. The sound is localized and directed towards your head rather than outward.
