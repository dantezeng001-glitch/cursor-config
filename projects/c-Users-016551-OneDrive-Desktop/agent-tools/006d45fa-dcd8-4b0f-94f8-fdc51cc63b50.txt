Specification

### What Bluetooth version does soundcore Aerofit support?

Bluetooth V5.3

### What audio codecs does soundcore Aerofit support?

SBC and AAC

### How many Bluetooth devices can the headphones record?

Up to 8 Bluetooth devices at one time.

### What is the Bluetooth range?

10m/33ft

### What is the waterproof grade of these headphones?

Headphones: IPX7 Charging case: Non-waterproof Note: - Do not place the earbuds and the charging case into a washing machine. - Make sure that the earbuds and the charging case are dry before charging.

### Does the charging case support wireless charging?

The charging case does not support wireless charging.

### Do the earbuds support fast charging?

The earbuds support fast charging, with 10 minutes of charging providing around 4 hours of battery life. The charging case does not support fast charging.

### Does the product have a wearing detection feature?

This product does not have a wearing detection feature.

### What is the maximum latency of the earbuds?

Around 200ms.

### What is the maximum sound pressure of the earbuds?

The maximum sound pressure is 88db.

Battery Related

### What is the battery capacity of the earbuds and how long does it take to charge them?

85mAh for each of the left and right earbuds, about 1 hour for a full charge.

### What is the battery capacity of the charging case and how long does it take to charge?

750mAh, about 2 hours for a full charge.

### How many times can I charge the earbuds with a full charge of the charging case?

The earbuds can be fully recharged a total of 2.8 times with a full-power charging case.

### How long is the battery life of the earbuds, and how long does the charging case last?

At 50% volume, the earbuds can last for about 11 hours when listening to music. With the charging case, the total battery life is around 42 hours.

### How long is the battery life of the earbuds during calls?

The earbuds can last for about 8.5 hours during calls.

### How many battery cycles do the earbuds have?

### What are the required specifications for charging the charging case?

A charger and charging cable with 5V/1A or above are required.

Operation and Usage

### Do the earbuds provide a voice prompt for power on and low battery?

The earbuds will give a low battery voice prompt when the battery level is at approximately 10%, and this will occur every 15 minutes.

### Are the earbuds suitable for use while diving or swimming?

No

### How to enter the multi-point pairing mode?

Go to the settings in the top right corner of the soundcore app, select 'Dual Connections', enable the function, and finally select 'Connect New Device'.

### What should I do if there is no sound after switching devices in multi-point pairing mode?

1. Ensure that one of the devices has paused music playback and is not occupying the audio channel. 2. Some devices may have compatibility issues, and switching devices can take around 20 seconds.

### How do I reset the earbuds?

- Delete the pairing history from your Bluetooth device first. - Place the earbuds in the charging case and leave the case open. Press and hold the reset button on the charging case for 10 seconds until the LED indicators on the charging case start to scroll and then stay on for 5 seconds. At this time, your earbuds have been successfully reset. When resetting the earbuds, make sure the earbuds are properly connected to the charging case, or it may cause the reset to fail. When you place the left and right earbuds in the charging case, there will be white LED indicators in the charging case which confirms the earbuds are properly connected. If the indicators in the charging case don't light up, please check whether the charging case and earbuds are properly connected, have enough power, and clean the charging pins with a dry cloth.

### How do I force the earbuds to pair?

- Place both earbuds back into the charging case, with the case lid open. - Press and hold the charging case button for 3 seconds. The charging case indicator light will start to scroll, and the earbuds will disconnect from the current connection and enter pairing mode. - Turn on Bluetooth on your device and then pair it with soundcore Aerofit.

### How do I turn off the earbuds?

1. When the earbuds are placed back into the charging case and the lid is closed, they will automatically power off and the Bluetooth connection will be disconnected. 2. If the earbuds are not connected to Bluetooth, they will automatically power off after 30 minutes. (The automatic power-off time can be customized in the soundcore app).

### Can I just use a single earbud?

Yes, you can use a single earbud. However, you cannot perform operations such as pairing, resetting, or customizing the EQ in mono mode.

### How do I adjust the volume via the earbuds?

The volume adjustment function is disabled by default, but you can enable via the soundcore app and customize the touch controls.

### How do I pair the left and right earbuds with different devices respectively?

The left and right earbuds do not support pairing with different devices.

### What should I do if the product becomes hot while charging?

- Stop charging and check if there is any moisture or sweat in the charging port of the charging case. - Try cleaning the charging port before attempting to charge again.

### What should I do if there is no sound coming from the earbuds during a call, but it is heard through the phone after the headphones are paired or reconnected with the phone?

Please select "soundcore Aerofit" as the output for calls on your phone.

### How do I maintain and care for the earbuds?

1. Do not leave the earbuds unused for a long time. It is recommended to use them at least once a month. The battery life can be significantly affected if the earbuds are left completely discharged. 2. Try not to use the earbuds until the remaining battery is only 20% or until it shuts down due to low battery before charging. This can also greatly impact the battery life. 3. The charging case should not be left in a low battery state for a long period of time. Please regularly charge the case. 4. Clean the charging contact pins promptly after use. 5. Store the earbuds on a dry desktop. If they accidentally come into contact with liquid, dry them with a hairdryer on the cool setting.

### What should I do if one side of the earbuds is malfunctioning, either by not powering on or failing to pair?

- Remove the protective film covering the charging pins on the earbuds. - Ensure that the earbuds are fully charged and powered on. - Reset the earbuds per the following steps: Place the earbuds in the charging case and leave the case open. Press and hold the reset button on the charging case for 10 seconds until the LED indicators on the charging case start to scroll and then stay on for 5 seconds. At this time, your earbuds have been successfully reset. - Try these earbuds with another device.

### How do I turn off the touch prompt tone?

You can turn on/off the prompt tone in the "More Settings" page of the soundcore app.

### How do I lock the touch control function with one click? (both function and prompt tone are locked with one click)

You can go to the Controls interface in the soundcore app, select the Settings icon in the upper right corner, and then turn on/off the Control Lock. Please note that after enabling the Control Lock function, the touch controls and touch prompt tone of the earbuds will be temporarily disabled.

At this time, your earbuds have been successfully reset.

### What should I do if I experience sound leakage?

First and foremost, the open-sound headphones are designed to deliver a more natural and expansive soundstage, coupled with a comfortable wearing experience. This design permits some ambient noise to seep in, allowing you to stay aware of your surroundings while indulging in your music. This feature proves particularly useful for outdoor activities or situations where it's crucial to be mindful of environmental sounds. However, a byproduct of this design is the "sound leakage" issue. It's not a flaw in the product's quality, but rather a natural occurrence associated with the open-sound design. This might result in others hearing some sound at high volumes, but under normal volumes, this should seldom happen.

Awesome!

First of all, I want to start by saying that before these, I'd never owned any soundcore products. Because I'd not heard much about the brand, I was a bit hesitant to try them, but because I got them almost 50% off and needed a pair of open-ear earbuds, I decided to give them a shot. I'm very glad that I did! I have some points that I'd like to touch on, to give more perspective:I had absolutely no issues with the fit with these, and I don't consider myself to have "small" ears. That being said, it does take some adjusting to get them to sit correctly on your ears comfortably. Not a big deal, but takes a couple extra seconds. The ear hooks are very comfortable and don't cause any fatigue or irritation. They're super lightweight, so much so that I found myself "flexing" my ears or lifting them, to try and hold the hooks on and keep them from falling off, although this really wasn't necessary, just something I had to get used to not doing, telling myself that they were secure and fine, and to relax my ears. The sound is great, and the app is nice because it shows you the battery percentage of each ear piece as well as the case. You can customize the sound with their EQ, and the sound is great for me without needing to do that. Some people said the sound sucked, well, it doesn't suck. It's actually great, considering the "kind" of earphones you're using. You're not going to get deep bass or rich, complex sound with open-ears. However, I did not notice a "huge" difference between the sound with these vs traditional earbuds. It was different, but not at all bad. I used them on the treadmill at the gym, and they're great for sound, I was still able to focus on the music and drown out some sounds. But if people were talking on the treadmills next to me, i could easily hear it and become distracted. I do think that these are better for outside activity, where you definitely need the situational awareness. I'm looking forward to using them while running outside when it warms up, because previously I was using a single earbud on my runs and didn't hear all of the sounds my songs had to offer because of it. Some other points- I find it odd that these are marketed as everyday earphones while the "pro" are marketed as the active version, when that's simply false. These are ipx7 rated whereas the pro are, I believe, ipx4 rated. Ipx4 isn't bad at all, but as a heavy sweater and a runner, I was definitely going for the ipx7 and they are also cheaper! So if you're a heavy sweater or plan to get these wet, get these instead and save yourself some money!Overall, I'd say this: these are pretty great but aren't practical in many situations. They're great for outdoor running or exercise. I probably wouldn't pay the retail price for them, but am extremely happy with them at the price I got them for. If you're in need of these type of earphones, these are an excellent option.
